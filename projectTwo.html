Background information

The project: Fitness applications have opened the ground to monitor users’ activity records. However, some users try to “cheat” on the system to either get undeserved rewards or to build a positive image on social media. Our goal of this porject is to design a system that can effectively manage fitness app users’ cheating behaviors to promote more physical activities.

design process

We first identify what causes users’ cheating behaviors on fitness apps as well as their mental models of doing physical exercises in general through secondary research and user interview.

Next, we identify one problem that is considered the major cause for cheating.

Then we design a solution based on data gathered from previously conducted research.

Finally we perform a usability testing to identify usability issues with our prototype.

1.1 secondary research - related papers about cheating behavior

Cheating on fitness apps can be originated from people’s cheating behaviors in gaming. People cheat in games, especially online games, for the purpose of getting undeserved rewards, and gratifying their vanity to make their names appear on the leaderboard. Similar to cheating in gaming, Gol-Oz & Zukerman (2015) stated that it is reasonable to assume that people’s cheating behaviors on fitness apps may carry similar intentions. Furthermore, cheating in gamified fitness apps may have some real-life implications, which may result in more intensive cheating behaviors.

Even though cheating is usually considered to have negative consequences(Duh & Chen, 2009; Wu & Chen, 2013 ), it is argued cheating can be conceptualized as having positive implications, if it eventually promotes physical activity (Gol-Oz & Zukerman, 2015; Lee & Lim, 2017 ). Lee & Lim (2017) proposed that instead of simply banning cheating, designers might want to balance cheating to more effective physical engagement to the users. Several researchers implied that integration of social aspects (Saksono et al., 2015) and temporality (Park 2013) to the system can be helpful to promote user engagement in using fitness apps for physical activities.
